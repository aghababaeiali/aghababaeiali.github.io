01 / About

From harassment datasets
to generative AI

My path into NLP started with a dataset about harassment, real stories shared by women, labeled by the degree of fear, shame, and toxicity they experienced. Working with BERT and RoBERTa to model those emotions was meaningful, but what really hooked me was the next question my professor asked: which words are actually driving the model's decisions, and by how much?

That question led me deep into explainability: Integrated Gradients, SHAP, LIME, and collaboration with psychologists to validate whether what the model found actually made human sense. It was the moment I realized NLP sits at a rare intersection of rigorous engineering and direct human relevance.

What excites me most now is using generative AI to understand text at scale, analyzing social media comments, surfacing patterns, and building systems that can respond intelligently based on real context. Work where a model doesn't just classify, but helps people make sense of what others are saying.

There's also a personal thread here. During my electrical engineering degree, research was slow and exhausting. When LLMs arrived during my master's, I watched that change overnight. That shift made me want to be on the side that builds these tools, not just uses them.

Explainable NLP with Integrated Gradients

Fine-tuned BERT and RoBERTa on a harassment dataset labeled with psychological markers (fear, shame, toxicity). Applied Integrated Gradients via Captum for token-level attribution, with SHAP and LIME for comparison. Validated results with psychologists. Contributed to a published arXiv paper.
