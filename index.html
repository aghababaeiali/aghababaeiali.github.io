01 / About

From Slurs dataset
to generative AI

My path into NLP started with the Slurs dataset, 336 real stories written by women describing experiences of verbal harassment, labeled by psychologists for shame, fear, and sexual objectification. Fine-tuning BERT and RoBERTa on it was meaningful work. But the question that really hooked me was: which words are actually driving the model's decisions, and by how much?

That led me to explainability, Integrated Gradients via Captum, compared against SHAP and DeepLIFT, with results validated by social psychologists. It was the moment I realized NLP sits at a rare intersection of rigorous engineering and direct human relevance.

What excites me most now is using generative AI to understand text at scale, analyzing social media comments, surfacing patterns, and building systems that can respond intelligently based on real context. Work where a model doesn't just classify, but helps people make sense of what others are saying.

There's also a personal thread here. During my electrical engineering degree, research was slow and exhausting. When LLMs arrived during my master's, I watched that change overnight. That shift made me want to be on the side that builds these tools, not just uses them.

02 / Projects

Selected work

Explainability · Research 2025

Explainable NLP with Integrated Gradients

Fine-tuned BERT, RoBERTa, and BERTAgent on the Slurs dataset, women's accounts of verbal harassment labeled for shame, fear, and sexual objectification. Applied Integrated Gradients via Captum for word-level attribution, benchmarked against Sequential IG, DeepLIFT, and GradientSHAP using comprehensiveness, sufficiency, and approximation error. Developed a custom rendering pipeline with SpaCy to handle negations and subword tokens. Validated findings with social psychologists, and applied the same method to a small-dataset scenario using deliberate overfitting to surface class-defining keywords. Submitted to IEEE Transactions on Affective Computing.

Fine-tuning · Deployment 2026

NLP Sentiment Analysis Pipeline

End-to-end sentiment classification built from scratch: data loading, text cleaning, tokenization, and fine-tuning DistilBERT on 50K IMDB reviews, hitting 88% accuracy & F1. Model hosted on Hugging Face Hub, served via FastAPI (JSON API + HTML UI), Dockerized, and live on Render with auto-redeploy on push. Includes a full pytest suite covering data, preprocessing, and API responses.

Generation · Fine-tuning 2026

Text Summarization Pipeline

Fine-tuned FLAN-T5-small on the Samsum conversational dataset using LoRA on Kaggle GPU, merging adapters before export for a lean, portable model. Inference uses engineered prompt prefixing to prevent speaker mix-ups, beam search decoding, and logs compression ratio per request. Served via FastAPI, Dockerized, and live as a Gradio demo on Hugging Face Spaces.

Multimodal · Vision-Language 2026

Image Captioning Application

Gradio app built around Salesforce/blip-image-captioning-base with two modes: upload a single image for an instant caption, or paste a webpage URL to scrape, filter, and batch-caption all images, exporting results as a downloadable captions.txt. Uses beam search with anti-repetition tuning to avoid degenerate outputs. Deployable on Hugging Face Spaces or Docker. Built as part of the IBM AI Developer Professional Certificate.
